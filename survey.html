Conclusions

Research Findings

Observations

Communities rely heavily on untreated groundwater. Waste is often burned or dumped openly. Basic hygiene awareness is present but inconsistent in practice.

Major Problems

Lack of systematic waste collection. High risk of water-borne diseases during monsoons. Inadequate sanitation in some public areas.

Recommendations

Establish community waste bins. Conduct monthly clean drinking water camps. Provide school-level hygiene kits.

Overall Health Score: 68%

While awareness is growing, infrastructural gaps need addressing for full community wellbeing.
