Personal Hygiene

Keep your body and clothing clean to reduce infection risk and improve confidence.

Quick Tips: Wash hands daily; keep clothes fresh; carry a small hygiene kit.

Common Mistakes: Skipping soap; sharing towels; wearing damp clothes.

Did You Know: Clean habits protect both you and the people near you.

WHO Recommendation: Use clean water and soap for routine personal hygiene.

Healthy Habits: Body care, clean clothes, private hygiene, skin health.

3 min read

Hand Washing

A simple habit that prevents many common stomach and respiratory infections.

Quick Tips: Wash before eating, after toilet use, and after public contact.

Common Mistakes: Rushing under water; missing fingertips; forgetting after coughing.

Did You Know: Twenty seconds of washing is a strong daily defense.

WHO Recommendation: Wash all hand surfaces with soap and clean water.

Healthy Habits: Clean palms, nails, wrists, and between fingers.

Oral Hygiene

Daily oral care protects teeth, gums, breath, and overall health.

Quick Tips: Brush twice; use fluoride toothpaste; rinse after meals.

Common Mistakes: Brushing too hard; ignoring bleeding gums; skipping dental checks.

Did You Know: Healthy gums support comfort, confidence, and nutrition.

WHO Recommendation: Brush regularly and seek dental care for pain or bleeding.

Healthy Habits: Teeth, gums, tongue, hydration, dental visits.

Hair Care

Clean hair and scalp reduce irritation, odor, and avoidable infections.

Quick Tips: Wash as needed; dry scalp well; avoid sharing combs.

Common Mistakes: Using harsh products; sleeping with wet hair; tight hairstyles.

Did You Know: Scalp health is part of personal hygiene.

WHO Recommendation: Keep hair and scalp clean using safe, gentle routines.

Healthy Habits: Scalp care, clean combs, dandruff awareness.

Nail Care

Short clean nails reduce germs that move from hands to food and face.

Quick Tips: Trim weekly; clean under nails; avoid nail biting.

Common Mistakes: Sharing clippers; cutting too deep; ignoring swelling.

Did You Know: Nails can carry dirt even when hands look clean.

WHO Recommendation: Keep nails short, smooth, and clean.

Healthy Habits: Trim, clean, moisturize, avoid biting.

Bathing

Bathing removes sweat, dust, and microbes while protecting skin comfort.

Quick Tips: Use mild soap; dry between toes; wear clean clothes.

Common Mistakes: Very hot water; damp towels; skipping feet.

Did You Know: Dry skin folds well to reduce fungal risk.

WHO Recommendation: Maintain regular bathing suited to climate and activity.

Healthy Habits: Skin care, towel hygiene, foot care.

Menstrual Hygiene

Safe menstrual care protects health, comfort, dignity, and school participation.

Quick Tips: Change products regularly; wash hands; dispose safely.

Common Mistakes: Using products too long; flushing pads; avoiding help for severe pain.

Did You Know: Menstrual health is normal healthcare, not shame.

WHO Recommendation: Use clean absorbents and safe disposal practices.

Healthy Habits: Cycle tracking, clean products, privacy, pain support.

Balanced Diet

A varied plate supports growth, energy, immunity, and focus.

Quick Tips: Include vegetables, fruit, grains, protein, and healthy fats.

Common Mistakes: Skipping meals; excess packaged snacks; too little water.

Did You Know: No single food creates health; patterns matter most.

WHO Recommendation: Eat a variety of foods and limit excess sugar, salt, and fat.

Healthy Habits: Colorful plate, protein, fiber, hydration.

Nutrition

Nutrients help the body build, repair, think, move, and fight illness.

Quick Tips: Choose whole foods; include protein; eat seasonal produce.

Common Mistakes: Only counting calories; ignoring micronutrients; fad diets.

Did You Know: Iron, calcium, iodine, vitamin D, and B12 deserve attention.

WHO Recommendation: Use diverse, nutrient-rich foods across the week.

Healthy Habits: Macro and micronutrients, meal balance.

Healthy Eating

Healthy eating is about regular, realistic choices rather than perfection.

Quick Tips: Eat slowly; plan snacks; keep water nearby.

Common Mistakes: Eating while distracted; skipping breakfast; sugary drinks.

Did You Know: Mindful eating can reduce overeating and stomach discomfort.

WHO Recommendation: Prefer fresh foods and safe preparation.

Healthy Habits: Meal timing, mindful eating, safe food.

Water Safety

Safe water prevents dehydration and water-borne disease.

Quick Tips: Use filtered or boiled water when safety is uncertain.

Common Mistakes: Drinking from unsafe sources; storing water uncovered.

Did You Know: Clear water is not always safe water.

WHO Recommendation: Use safely treated water for drinking and cooking.

Healthy Habits: Purification, storage, hydration.

Physical Exercise

Quick Tips: Walk, stretch, play, cycle, dance, or practice sport.

Common Mistakes: Doing too much too fast; ignoring pain; long sitting.

Did You Know: Short movement breaks count when repeated consistently.

WHO Recommendation: Aim for regular activity and muscle strengthening.

Healthy Habits: Cardio, strength, flexibility, balance.

Mental wellbeing supports relationships, study, sleep, and resilience.

Quick Tips: Talk to someone; rest; move; write thoughts down.

Common Mistakes: Hiding distress; comparing online; delaying support.

Did You Know: Seeking help is a sign of strength.

WHO Recommendation: Support mental health through connection, rest, and professional care when needed.

Stress can be managed with breath, planning, movement, and support.

Quick Tips: Try box breathing; break tasks into steps; take short pauses.

Common Mistakes: All-nighters; doom scrolling; ignoring body signals.

Did You Know: Slow breathing can calm the nervous system quickly.

WHO Recommendation: Use healthy coping strategies and seek help for persistent distress.

Healthy Habits: Breathing, planning, boundaries, recovery.

Quick Tips: Keep regular sleep time; dim lights; reduce screens.

Common Mistakes: Caffeine late; scrolling in bed; irregular schedules.

Did You Know: Consistent wake time is a powerful sleep anchor.

Quick Tips: Follow local schedules; keep records; ask health workers.

Common Mistakes: Missing boosters; trusting myths; delaying child vaccines.

Did You Know: Vaccination protects individuals and communities.

WHO Recommendation: Stay up to date with recommended immunization.

Disease Prevention

Daily prevention lowers exposure to infections and health risks.

Quick Tips: Wash hands; cover coughs; use safe food and water.

Common Mistakes: Self-medicating antibiotics; ignoring fever; unsafe storage.

Did You Know: Prevention is easier than treatment.

Clean surroundings reduce pests, odors, injury, and disease spread.

Quick Tips: Dispose waste safely; clean shared spaces; avoid stagnant water.

Common Mistakes: Open dumping; clogged drains; uncovered bins.

Did You Know: A clean environment supports mental calm too.

WHO Recommendation: Maintain safe sanitation and waste practices.

Healthy Habits: Waste, drains, public spaces, sanitation.

Waste Management

Proper waste handling protects air, water, soil, and community health.

Quick Tips: Separate wet and dry waste; cover bins; compost when possible.

Common Mistakes: Burning plastic; mixing sharp waste; littering.

Did You Know: Waste becomes safer when sorted at the source.

WHO Recommendation: Dispose waste safely and follow local rules.

Healthy Habits: Segregation, compost, safe disposal.

Mosquito Prevention

Mosquito control prevents diseases such as dengue and malaria.

Quick Tips: Empty stagnant water; use nets; cover containers.

Common Mistakes: Ignoring coolers, pots, and tires; evening exposure.

Did You Know: Mosquitoes can breed in very small water collections.

WHO Recommendation: Remove breeding sites and use bite protection.

Healthy Habits: Stagnant water, nets, repellents.

Eye Care

Eye comfort improves learning, screen use, and daily functioning.

Quick Tips: Use the 20-20-20 rule; adjust light; blink often.

Common Mistakes: Screens too close; poor lighting; ignoring headaches.

Did You Know: Dry eyes are common during long screen sessions.

WHO Recommendation: Take regular breaks from close work and screens.

Healthy Habits: Screen breaks, lighting, checkups.

Digital Wellness

Quick Tips: Set screen breaks; mute nonessential alerts; protect bedtime.

Common Mistakes: Scrolling late; multitasking always; comparing online.

Did You Know: Digital rest is part of mental health.

WHO Recommendation: Balance screen time with sleep, activity, and connection.

Screen Time

Balanced screen use supports focus without blocking rest or movement.

Quick Tips: Schedule breaks; stand often; keep devices away at meals.

Common Mistakes: Endless autoplay; no breaks; screens before sleep.

Did You Know: Breaks improve both eyes and posture.

WHO Recommendation: Limit sedentary time and protect sleep routines.

Healthy Habits: Breaks, limits, movement, focus.

Posture

Good posture reduces neck, back, shoulder, and wrist strain.

Quick Tips: Keep screen at eye level; feet flat; shoulders relaxed.

Common Mistakes: Hunching; laptop on bed; no movement breaks.

Did You Know: The best posture is the next posture: move often.

WHO Recommendation: Use ergonomic habits and regular movement.

Healthy Habits: Desk setup, stretching, body awareness.

First Aid

Basic first aid helps people respond calmly before professional help arrives.

Quick Tips: Know emergency numbers; clean small wounds; learn CPR basics.

Common Mistakes: Putting substances on burns; moving injured people carelessly.

Did You Know: Fast calm action can reduce harm.

WHO Recommendation: Seek urgent care for severe symptoms or injury.

Healthy Habits: Wounds, burns, CPR awareness, emergency help.

Emergency Tips

Emergency readiness helps families act quickly and clearly.

Quick Tips: Save 112, 108, local doctor, and family contacts.

Common Mistakes: Panicking; delaying help; giving food to unconscious people.

Did You Know: A prepared contact list saves time.

WHO Recommendation: Call emergency services for life-threatening symptoms.

Healthy Habits: Numbers, first response, safety, calm action.
