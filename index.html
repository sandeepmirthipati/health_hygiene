Myth Busting

myths vs Facts

Flip each card to reveal the truth behind common health myths. Knowledge is your best medicine!

Myth

"Cold weather and getting wet in rain causes cold and flu."

Click to see the fact
Fact

Viruses cause cold and flu not cold temperatures. You can catch flu in summer too. Viruses spread person-to-person, not through weather.

Myth

"Vaccines cause autism and are dangerous for children."

Click to see the fact
Fact

No scientific evidence supports this. The original claim was from a fraudulent, retracted study. WHO, CDC, and 1.2M+ child studies confirm vaccines are safe.

Myth

"Eating as little as possible is the fastest way to lose weight."

Click to see the fact
Fact

Severe caloric restriction slows metabolism, causes muscle loss, and leads to nutrient deficiencies. A modest deficit + exercise + balanced diet is the right approach.

Myth

"Mental illness is a personal weakness or character flaw."

Click to see the fact
Fact

Mental illness is a medical condition with biological, genetic, and environmental causes just like diabetes or asthma. Seeking help is a sign of strength.

Myth

"Antibiotics are effective for treating cold, flu, and viral fevers."

Click to see the fact
Fact

Antibiotics only kill bacteria, NOT viruses. Misusing them creates drug-resistant superbugs one of WHO's top global health threats. Never self-medicate with antibiotics.

Myth

"Everyone needs exactly 8 glasses of water per day no more, no less."

Click to see the fact
Fact

Water needs vary by age, weight, activity, and climate. The best indicator is urine color; pale yellow often means good hydration. Adults generally need 2.5-3.5L daily.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should I drink every day?
+
Adults generally need 2.5-3.5 litres (8-12 glasses) of water per day. However, needs vary by activity level, climate, and body weight. The best indicator is urine color; pale yellow usually means you're well hydrated. Drink more during exercise, hot weather, or illness. Start with a glass of water first thing in the morning.
What is the right way to wash hands?
+
WHO recommends 6 steps: (1) Wet hands with clean water, (2) Apply soap to cover all surfaces, (3) Rub palms together, (4) Rub back of each hand, (5) Interlace fingers to clean between, (6) Rinse under running water and dry with clean towel. Total time should be minimum 20 seconds about the time to hum "Happy Birthday" twice.
How much sleep do I actually need?
+
According to the National Sleep Foundation: adults need 7-9 hours, teenagers 8-10 hours, and children ages 6-12 years need 9-12 hours. Consistency matters as much as duration; sleeping at the same time every day, including weekends, supports your circadian rhythm. Long periods without sleep can seriously impair alertness and reaction time.
How often should I exercise, and what type?
+
WHO recommends at least 150-300 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week, like brisk walking or cycling, or 75-150 minutes of vigorous activity. Add muscle-strengthening exercises 2 days/week. Even short 10-minute sessions count. If you're sedentary, start with 10-minute walks and gradually increase. Any movement is better than none.
What foods should I eat for better immunity?
+
Key immunity-boosting foods include: Vitamin C rich foods (amla, lemon, bell peppers), Zinc-rich foods (pumpkin seeds, chickpeas), Garlic and ginger (antimicrobial), Turmeric (anti-inflammatory), Vitamin D sources (fatty fish, egg yolks, sunlight), fermented foods (curd, idli) for gut health, and colorful vegetables for antioxidants. Remember: no single food boosts immunity it's the overall diet pattern that matters.
How do I manage stress and anxiety naturally?
+
Evidence-based natural approaches include: Regular exercise (as effective as medication for mild depression), Mindfulness meditation (10 min daily reduces anxiety by 30%), Deep breathing exercises (4-7-8 technique), Quality sleep, Social connection, Time in nature (proven cortisol reducer), Journaling, Limiting news and social media, and Eating a balanced diet. If symptoms are severe or persistent, please consult a mental health professional it's a sign of strength, not weakness.
